VCSearch: Bridging the Gap Between Well-Defined and Ill-Defined Problems in Mathematical Reasoning

VCSearch studies a failure mode that standard math benchmarks largely ignore: real-world mathematical problems with missing or contradictory conditions. It introduces a new benchmark, PMC, and a training-free formal-search framework that improves rejection of unsolvable problems without giving up performance on well-defined ones.

Why This Matters

Existing math reasoning systems are usually judged on carefully specified questions, but educational and real-world settings often contain incomplete or contradictory statements. A useful system should know when to reject.

5,000+ validated ill-defined math problems in PMC
+12% minimum gain in unsolvable-problem identification
8 benchmark subsets across Missing and Contra types
Training-Free framework using SMT-Lib and Z3 instead of fine-tuning
Problem

Well-Defined vs. Ill-Defined Reasoning

VCSearch starts from a practical observation: LLMs often produce fluent but wrong answers when a math word problem is missing key conditions or contains internal contradictions.

The paper contrasts ordinary solvable math word problems with two ill-defined categories: Missing-type problems, where necessary information is absent, and Contra-type problems, where the conditions contradict each other. Standard prompting often hallucinates answers instead of rejecting these cases.
Missing-type Contra-type Reject when unsolvable Avoid plausible hallucinations
Benchmark

PMC: Problems with Missing and Contradictory Conditions

PMC is built by mutating four common math reasoning datasets and validating the resulting problems as truly unsolvable. The public Hugging Face release exposes missing_test and contra_test splits.

01

Seed Datasets

PMC is constructed from GSM8K, SVAMP, AddSub, and MultiArith.

02

Two Ill-Defined Modes

Missing-type removes necessary conditions; Contra-type injects conflicting conditions.

03

Validation Pipeline

Candidate problems are checked by multiple LLMs, with disputed cases reviewed by humans.

04

Evaluation Metrics

R-Rate measures rejection; R-Score measures mixed-setting utility.

Method

Variable-Constraint Search

Instead of forcing an LLM to formalize the whole problem in one pass, VCSearch decomposes the formalization process into a dynamic search over variables and constraints.

VCSearch begins from an anchored initialization state, then repeatedly runs Preparation, Exploration, and Verification over a head variable. SMT solving and LLM-based judging are used together to refine the formal modeling state.
I

Anchored Initialization

Build a draft formal state and fall back to a variable-only anchor when constraints fail.

II

Preparation

Select one unexplored head variable and its related constraints to localize reasoning.

III

Exploration

Use the LLM to polish constraints and discover implicit variables that were missed before.

IV

Verification

Use Z3 and an LLM judge to compare states and keep the better formalization branch.
